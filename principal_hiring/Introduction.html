Candidates' Task

The candidates took part in an earlier session and were asked to solve a series of problems. The problems all have the same structure.

The following is an example of the problem: one needs to "convert" the numbers reported in the lower panel into letters according to the conversion table in the upper panel.

In this example, the solution to this problem is: MDQEP

The candidates had {{time}} seconds to solve as many problems as they could. They earned {{piece_rate}} for every problem they solved correctly.

Candidates' Beliefs

Each candidate was matched with another candidate and was asked to estimate the probability that he/she performed better than the other in the task.

The closer the probability that they reported to the actual outcome of the comparison, the higher the likelihood of earning {{C.EARN_BEL}}. We conceived a system of incentives that encouraged a truthful report of beliefs.
