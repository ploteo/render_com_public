Candidates' Task

The candidates participated in an earlier session and were asked to solve a series of problems. The problems all have the same structure.

The following is an example of the problem: one needs to "convert" the numbers reported in the lower panel into letters according to the conversion table in the upper panel.

In this example, the solution to this problem is: MDQEP

The candidates had {{time}} seconds to solve as many problems as they could. They earned {{piece_rate}} for every problem they solved correctly.

The candidates you will be viewing were paired due to their comparable performance in the task.

Candidates' Beliefs

Each candidate was matched with another candidate who and was asked to estimate the probability that he/she correctly solved more problems than the other one (that is, their beliefs).

The closer the probability that they reported to the actual outcome of the comparison, the higher the likelihood of earning {{C.EARN_BEL}}. We conceived a system of monetary incentives to encourage them to report their beliefs truthfully.
